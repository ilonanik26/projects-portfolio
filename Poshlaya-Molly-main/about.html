Poshlaya Molly

Poshlaya Molly was officially founded in 2016, but the band's roots go back to 2014, when friends Kirill Bledny and Bogdan Stupka started making music together.

“At first, only friends came to the shows — then suddenly, over 400 fans filled the room.”

Their debut album, "8 способов как бросить дрочить" (8 Ways to Stop Masturbating), released in 2017, quickly gained traction, especially through VKontakte and social media.

“The album ranked #2 in The Flow's best Russian albums of 2017.”

In 2018, they released the EP "Sad girl with eyes like a dog", showcasing a more emotional and experimental sound.

“The EP made it to #12 on The Flow's best Russian albums of 2018.”

In 2020, the band released the "Paycheck" EP, including a surprise collaboration with former SEREBRO member Katya Kischuk.

“Tracks like ‘Мишка’ blended pop-punk with electronic influences — a new direction.”

After a 2-year break, Poshlaya Molly returned in 2024 with the EP "RATT#WHORE", a bold comeback with mature themes and evolved sound.

“The band's style is rawer, darker — but still very much Poshlaya Molly.”
